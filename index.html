PYTHON

Today we will take a look at basics at python.


Variables

In Python, a variable is like a box where you can store things. Imagine you have a box with a label on it, and inside the box, you can keep different items like toys, books, or snacks. In Python, you can use variables to store information, like numbers or words. For example, if you want to keep track of your age, you can create a variable called age and store your age in it. Later, you can use that variable to do math or show your age on the screen!


    Integer

    These are whole numbers, like 5, 12, or -3. You use integers when you want to count things or do math with whole numbers.

    String

    A string is a type of variable that stores words or sentences. It’s like a piece of text! You can use strings to store your name, a sentence, or even a fun quote. Strings are always wrapped in quotation marks, either single (') or double (").

    Float

    Floats are numbers that can have decimals, like 3.14 or 9.99. If you need to work with numbers that aren't whole, like prices or measurements, you use floats.
